Manner of Holding
The manner of holding is the type of property ownership for the flat. It determines how the property will be passed on if one of the owners passes away.
Read more on Manner of Holding
What is your preferred holding type?
Joint Tenancy
The co-owners have an equal shares in the flat.
The property automatically goes to the remaining co-owner(s) if you pass on, regardless of whether you have a will or not
Tenancy-in-Common
Each co-owner holds a separate and distinct shares in the flat.
The property does not go automatically to the remaining co-owner(s) if you pass on
You can decide how you want to distribute your share of the property in your will
How do you want to split your shares?
Indicate how many shares each applicant would hold, under tenancy-in-commons.
Equal distribution among applicants
Each co-owner receives an equal share, with each having 1 part of the shares.
Specify individual shares among applicants
Co-owners can hold varying amounts of shares, totalling 100 shares.
Allocation of shares
Each applicant must have at least 1 share. Total share must add up to 100.
